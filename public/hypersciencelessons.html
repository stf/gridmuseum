Lesson 1 — Easy Home Cooking

Wednesday, May 16th 6:30-8:30pm




Foundation Menu

Fresh chopped salad with quick mustard vinaigrette

Seasonal fresh vegetables provide a wealth of vitamines, minerals, and complex phytochemicals, including antioxidants (anthocynins, polyphenols, etc.) which are essential for healthy cell function. A well balanced, crunchy salad serves as a textual counterpoint to the baked pasta dish, while the acid in a tangy vinaigrette cuts through the creamy sauce, keeping the flavors from dulling as the meal progresses.


Creamy bacon macaroni and cheese (vegetarian optional)

Rich and satisfying, this dish contains slow release carbs (low on the glycemic index, which prevents spikes in blood sugar), moderate amounts of fat, mostly unsaturated (important for flavor and as part of hunger satiation), and is a wonderful source of calcium, fiber, vitamins A, C, E, & B6, potassium, manganese and assorted minerals.


Flaky buttermilk drop biscuits

Made with a whole wheat flour, this high fiber basic biscuit is a staple item—incredibly fast and easy to prepare, great with modifications (fresh herbs, cheese, diced vegetables), and perfect as a biscuit topping for casseroles.
